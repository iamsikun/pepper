\begin{abstract}
Factor models are central to asset pricing and portfolio construction, yet dominant models such as Fama-French are designed for equities and explain little of the return variation in bonds, REITs, and emerging market assets. Deep latent factor models improve in-sample fit but typically produce abstract, non-tradable factors that resist economic interpretation. We propose the Autoencoding Factor Model (AFM), a neural autoencoder whose bottleneck layer is a set of explicit portfolio returns: each latent factor is the return of a tradable portfolio whose weights are generated by a neural network conditioned on mutual fund characteristics---sector holdings and asset class allocations from regulatory filings---and a learned market-state embedding from past returns. Trained end-to-end on approximately 1{,}000 US mutual funds spanning 10 asset classes, the AFM with five factors achieves 85.0\% average out-of-sample $R^2$, a 16.6 percentage point improvement over the Fama-French four-factor model. The largest gains arise on non-equity assets where traditional factors have near-zero explanatory power: US aggregate bonds (84\% vs.\ 11\%), emerging market debt (44\% vs.\ 11\%), and high yield (53\% vs.\ 35\%).
\end{abstract}
